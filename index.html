Hack-Ed

Hack for Education

What is Hack-Ed

Hack-Ed is a global event for all of the problem-solvers out there. It's a hackathon initiated by Leapfrog to bring all the bright minds from around the world together, to collaboratively innovative solutions for today’s educational challenges.

Why Hack-Ed

Due to the recent situation of the world, one of the major industries that suffered was the education industry. Especially in the countries that are lagging a bit behind on technological aspects, the education sector has taken a major hit. One cannot help but wonder, there must be a solution to this crisis.
In this virtual Hackathon, we want you to join us and brainstorm ideas as well as prototype innovative solutions to this very problem

The Challenge

  • Build solutions for today’s education problems.

  • Innovate alternative learning environments and tools.

  • Get creative and present prototypes with a multidisciplinary approach.

What's in it for you?

Hack-Ed is not only an opportunity to showcase your ideas, but it will also act as a platform for you to build your skills, meet like-minded people, and grow your network.
Your idea may have the power to bring a significant impact, globally.
Since we value your efforts and time, and this is a competition, one best idea will be awarded the prize of $1000

Rules

Please keep the following rules in mind throughout the hackathon period. These rules are not subject to change and should be followed strictly as-is throughout the event.

Time and date

  • The maximum number of members in a team is 4, however, only one prize can be received per product. [A team can have 1 to at most 4 members. The prize will be given to the winning team.]

  • On 26th June 2020, you can start working on your idea.

  • You will have to submit your Actions by 28th June 2020.

  • On orientation day, at least one of your team members need to be present.

  • The presentation will be held [on the 29th June 2020 at xx am/pm]. Your whole team needs to be present.

  • The presentation will be for 15 minutes. You will be given 8 minutes to present your idea and your product. And for the 7 minutes, you will be receiving QnAs from the judges.

  • On the presentation day, your whole team needs to attend.

  • Plagiarism of the product from open platforms or others will not entertained.

  • If Nepalese participants win the competition then they will be presented the winner amount according to the closing day’s exchange rate.

Eligibility

Your submission must:

  • be you/your team's original product.

  • not violate the intellectual property rights or other rights including but not limited to the copyright, trademark, patent, contract, and/or privacy rights, of any other person or entity.

Platforms

A submitted application must run on at least one of the following:

  • a smartphone or tablet(iOS, Android or Windows Mobile)

  • a web browser (mobile or desktop)

  • a desktop computer (Windows PC, Linux, Mac OS Chromebook)

  • other hardware (including but not limited to, wearable technology, open-source hardware, or proprietary hardware

Who can participate?
Developers, designers, hardware hackers, recent college grads, blockchain enthusiasts, and anyone who wants to leverage technology and build their ideas. We welcome educators, instructors, and researchers who want to make a difference in education with the use of modern technology. here

Is solo participation allowed?
Yes, you can participate individually and in groups. Please sign up here

I don’t have any idea but would like to pick a team after pitching the idea.
Please mention your strengths in the signup form so that we can match you with a team that needs your expertise.

Our product is already on the market, can we still join?
No, if the product is already on the market you can’t propose the same idea to the hackathon.

What’s the team size?
The maximum team size is 4.

I am not a developer, can I join the event?
Yes, you can, we welcome educators and researchers to join in and help the teams out.

Will there be any mentors during the event?
We cannot promise you mentors but if we have one, we will mention it during the orientation. A design thinking session will be conducted as part of orientation and a resource person will be available every day for an hour.

What is the cost of participation?
There is no participation fee for Hack-ed.

I am not in Kathmandu, can I join?
There is no restriction on languages or the choice of technology. However, we expect teams and individuals to be able to push to a public GitHub repository. Start here if you are new to GitHub.

What technology should I use?
Yes, of course, you can. Both submission and presentation will be done online.

Who owns the project and intellectual property for submitted projects?
The team that develops the project is the owner of the project and intellectual property (IP). We do encourage sharing of code with others in the community or making the code open source for the time period of the event.

Does my team need to be online during the hackathon?
We won’t need the entire team to be online at all times. However, we will need one member from your team to be available during the event so that we can contact you if needed.
